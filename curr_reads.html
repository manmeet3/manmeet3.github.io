Previous

  • Economics in One Lesson by Hentry Hazlitt

    Quick Summary: This book very concisely states that many economical fallacies exist resulting from special interest bias and a neglect of the long-term consequences and their associated varied outcomes. The lesson itself can be best quoted as "The art of economics consists in looking not merely at the immediate but at the longer effects of any act or policy; it consists in tracing the consequences of that policy not merely for one group but for all groups".

  • A Short History of Nearly Everything by Bill Bryson

    Quick Summary: As the title suggests, this one is about a quick glimse into how, we as a civilization, came to be. It does a great job at presenting information in simple language and digestable chunks. Each section within deals with one sphere of inquiry such as outter space, the Earth and organic life. Along the way, the author sheds light on the inspirational lives led by the scientists and researchers.
